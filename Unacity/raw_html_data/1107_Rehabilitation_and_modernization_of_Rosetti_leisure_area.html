Rehabilitation and modernization of Rosetti leisure area

Reabilitare și modernizare zona de agrement C.A Rosetti
+−
Leaflet | © OpenStreetMap contributors
Iași, Iaşi (FUA), Romania
City population: 342738
Duration: 2020 – ongoing
Implementation status: Ongoing
Scale: Micro-scale: District/neighbourhood level
Project area: 16158 m2
Type of area: Vacant or abandoned land
Last updated: October 2024

Iasi has limited access to green-blue open spaces and fails to meet national and European standards for per capita green space and proximity to larger green areas. Additionally, the city experiences significant fragmentation in its green spaces. To address these issues, the municipality of Iasi launched a project in 2020 to restore a vacant and degraded area, transforming it into a park. Funded by European resources, the project aims to rehabilitate the neglected land by constructing a green park and introducing local vegetation. The primary goal is to provide a leisure and relaxation space for local citizens and visitors while improving the quality of the surrounding air by converting much of the terrain into green space. The vacant land is situated in the historic Ticau neighborhood, which has been affected by municipal neglect, characterized by poor infrastructure and limited connectivity to the city's main areas. Furthermore, the project aims to reduce the heat island effect by creating green oases in a city marked by many “hard surfaces,” such as paved areas, which contribute to this phenomenon expected to intensify in the coming years. Originally scheduled for completion by the end of 2022, the project has been delayed due to a lack of funds and is now expected to be finished by the end of 2024 (Refs 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 7, 11).

Project objectives

To increase the availability of green space per capita in Iasi to meet national and European standards and address inequality in access to these spaces. To improve access to green spaces by ensuring a higher percentage of the population lives within 300 meters of a green area of at least 0.5 hectares. To rehabilitate and transform abandoned and degraded areas into functional green spaces, such as parks, enhancing urban livability. To enhance air quality by converting vacant land into green spaces that serve as natural air filters. To reduce the urban heat island effect by creating new green areas. (Ref. 1, 2, 3, 4)

Implementation activities

The majority of the area will be converted into green spaces, transforming approximately 1.61 hectares of the total 1.79-hectare site into vibrant ecological zones (Refs 1-4). The process will begin by clearing the existing vegetation to prepare the land for further development and improvement (Ref 4, p.70). Following this, land modeling will be conducted to create a suitable landscape that accommodates both planting and recreational activities (Ref 4, p.70). To establish durable green spaces, perennial plants and turf will be planted across the surfaces of the area (Ref 4, p.70). Finally, the project will include the planting of trees and shrubs to enhance the greenery and biodiversity of the site (Ref 4, p.70).

Details on the roles of the organisations involved in the project

The City Hall of the municipality of Iasi initiated the project and took a leading role in its implementation by forming a partnership with a local company responsible for executing the project. Governance arrangements included online and physical consultations with local communities that included discussions on design of the park. Additionally, the City Hall established partnerships with community organizations representing women, young people, and people with disabilities and discussed future plans for involvement in park's maintenance for underrepresented groups. The project is co-financed by the EU (Refs 4, 6,7)
