Horvath-hill recreational green area development

Aktív rekreációs zöldterület fejlesztése a Horváth-tetőn

Last updated: October 2024

The "Active Recreational Green Area Development at Horváth-tető" in Miskolc is a project aimed at revitalizing a central yet underutilized urban space, commissioned by the Municipality of Miskolc in 2017. A distinctive feature of Miskolc is the Avas hill, located in the city center, which beautifully blends nature with urban elements. Despite its potential, the area had been neglected, particularly following the decline of vineyard culture and the reduced use of old wine cellars. To address this, a comprehensive development plan was initiated to rejuvenate the Avas region, with Horváth-tető at its core. The project aimed to transform the area into an outdoor recreational hub for all age groups. The hill's historic terraces, once popular promenades, were repurposed into distinct, multifunctional spaces. Sports enthusiasts can enjoy facilities such as street workout stations, an outdoor gym, a skate park, a climbing wall, and parkour elements. For those looking to relax, picnic areas, sunbathing spots, and a retro-style terrace are available. The entire development prioritizes accessibility, providing inclusive options for visitors with mobility challenges. The park also features significant landscaping, including the planting of trees, shrubs, perennials, ornamental grasses, and ferns, enhancing its overall appeal. Infrastructure improvements were made, including the restoration of stairs, paths, retaining walls, and the rainwater drainage system. A special shallow basin was designed to collect rainwater and support plant roots while also serving as a playful area for dogs within the dog park. Since its opening in 2022, the park has become a popular destination, offering a tranquil escape in a beautiful setting. It provides a wide range of recreational opportunities for people of all ages, making it a valuable addition to Miskolc’s urban landscape and connecting the historical Avas hill with modern city life.(Ref.1,2)

Project objectives

To create a multifunctional recreational space that caters to a wide range of age groups (including people with mobility issues) and interests while emphasizing environmental sustainability and greening efforts. To enhance green spaces through the planting of evergreen trees, shrubs, perennials, and 143 ferns. To promote sustainable landscaping and improve soil conditions by using pine bark for mulching and renovating 1,414 m² of lawn, along with landscaping 2,884 m² of grassed areas. To improve accessibility and usability with stabilized gravel pavement, natural stone paving, and rubber flooring to create safe and durable surfaces. To support social interactions and physical activities by installing playground elements, gym/workout stations, and a complete skate park, as well as dog fitness elements. To boost public infrastructure with bicycle stands, drinking fountains, and the construction of custom railings, street furniture, and a lookout point. To manage rainwater by creating a shallow basin in the dog park. (Ref.1,2)

Implementation activities

The Municipality of Miskolc hired Mento Kft. to implement a variety of enhancements in the area. The project included the installation of 1,091 m² of stabilized gravel pavement and 88 m² of natural stone pavement. Additionally, 1,751.5 m² of poured rubber flooring was added. As part of the landscaping efforts, the project involved planting 6 evergreen trees, 57 tall shrubs, 276 medium-height shrubs, and 2,689 low and ground-cover shrubs. Furthermore, 7,092 perennials, 376 ornamental grasses, and 143 ferns were planted. To promote sustainability, 1,800 m² of pine bark was spread, while 1,414 m² of lawn was renovated and 2,884 m² of grass area was landscaped. The project also focused on recreational facilities, incorporating 39 different playground elements, 15 gym/workout elements, and 19 dog fitness elements, all based on technical specifications. Public infrastructure improvements included 97 pieces of street furniture (benches, information posts, waste bins, tables), 277 linear meters of custom railing, 4 drinking fountains, and 22 bicycle stands. Additional features included 3 rain shelters, 1 lookout point, and the installation of 25 m of drainage and 81 m of drinking water pipeline. Five water meter-equipped manholes were constructed, along with 61 lampposts designed according to technical specifications. The project culminated in the creation of a complete skate park and a climbing wall, both designed according to production plans. (Ref.1)

Details on the roles of the organisations involved in the project

Miskolc Megyei Jogú Város Önkormányzata (Miskolc Municipality) served as the main project initiator and overseer. Mento Kft. was responsible for the implementation and construction of the project. The European Union acted as a co-financier through the European Regional Development Fund, while the Hungarian State provided additional financial contributions alongside the EU. (Ref.1,2,3,5)
