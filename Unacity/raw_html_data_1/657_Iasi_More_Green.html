Iasi More Green

Iasi Mai Verde

Implementation status: Completed
Scale: Sub-microscale: Street scale (including buildings)
Project area: unknown
Type of area: Waste disposal site or landfill
Last updated: March 2025

In 2022 an afforestation initiative was implemented near a landfill located in the Tutora commune (1 - 6). Organized by the City Hall of Tutora, the event brought together representatives from the regional government of Iasi County, the "Asociația de Dezvoltare Intercomunitară pentru Salubritate" (ADIS), and volunteers from SC Girexim SA, SC Salubris Iasi, and Edil Industri Iasi. Together, they planted 2,000 acacia saplings, donated by the Forestry Directorate of Iasi (6). The afforestation action took place under the umbrella of the regional environmental campaign "Iasi Mai Verde" (Iasi More Green), led by the County Council of Iasi. This campaign promotes a range of eco-friendly activities, including educational programs in schools, waste cleanup events, and afforestation initiatives in partnership with ADIS, while actively encouraging the support and participation of local municipalities from across the region (1, 3, & 7). "Iasi Mai Verde" aligns with national environmental strategies such as the Ministry of the Environment's "Curățăm România" (Let's Clean Romania) and the annual Planting Forest Month (3 & 6). On the same day, as part of the Iasi Mai Verde initiative, students from a local school in Tutora were given a practical education workshop on how waste management operates at the regional level. The students were also invited to witness the afforestation action and participate, fostering a deeper understanding of environmental stewardship (1- 5). The newly planted trees are acting as a natural barrier, and help improve air quality by filtering dust, pollution, and waste particles originating from the nearby Tutora landfill. In turn, this contributes to better living conditions for the residents in close proximity to the landfill, enhancing both their health and overall quality of life (5).

Project objectives

To improve air quality and mitigate odors from the landfill, enhancing the living conditions of residents nearby (5). To protect the environment through afforestation (a "forest curtain") and waste cleanup initiatives (1 - 3 & 7). To raise awareness about the importance of nature and its role in limiting climate change (1 to 3 & 7). To educate younger generations on environmental topics, climate change, and waste management, fostering a deeper appreciation of nature, increase the engagement level within the community and bring people together to interact (5 & 7). To foster regional partnerships that engage public institutions in ecological actions and create new management approaches (1 & 3 & 7).

Implementation activities

A large-scale afforestation effort was carried out with the plantation of 2,000 acacia saplings by volunteers from a broad partnership that included local public institutions such as the Iasi County Council, Tutora City Hall, and the Asociația de Dezvoltare Intercomunitară pentru Salubritate (ADIS), as well as private companies responsible for managing the nearby landfill (SC Girexim SA, SC Salubris Iași, and Edil Industri Iași) (1 to 6). In addition to the afforestation, an educational workshop on waste management processes was organized for 50 students from Școala Gimnazială Țuțora. The students were also invited to participate in and observe the plantation, giving them hands-on insight into the importance of environmental actions and waste management (2, 3 & 5). Also within the framework of this campaign, numerous guided tours were organized for students from the county at the Țuțora Integrated Waste Management Center, in the form of interactive lessons on waste collection and recycling. So far, more than 500 children have signed up for these activities. (3)

Details on the roles of the organisations involved in the project

The educational workshop was carried out under the "Iasi Mai Verde" campaing organised by the Iasi County Hall (regional body) in partnership with ADIS (Asociația de Dezvoltare Intercomunitară pentru Salubritate, a public institution) and the City Hall of Tutora invited them to carry out and afforestation action additionally (1 - 6). Volunteers from SC Girexim SA, SC Salubris Iași and Edil Industri Iași (all three are private companies) and the public institutions helped carrying out the plantation together with students from Școala Gimnazială Țuțora that attended the workshops (2 & 5 & 6). The saplings were donated by the Iasi Foresty Directorate (regional body) (6).
