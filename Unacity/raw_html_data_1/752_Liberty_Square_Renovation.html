Liberty Square Renovation

Námestie Slobody Renovácia
+−
Leaflet | © OpenStreetMap contributors
Bratislava, Slovakia
City population: 410016
Duration: 2015 – 2017
Implementation status: Completed
Scale: Micro-scale: District/neighbourhood level
Project area: 10000 m2
Type of area: Central Business District / City Centre
Last updated: October 2021

The present intervention is about the rehabilitation of the freedom square (Námestie hraničiarov) (formerly Gottwald square) in the city district of Petržalka of the city of Bratislava (9, 11). The area of the intervention is about 1 ha. The pavement was replaced by grass areas, trees, and flower beds in the square’s alleys. A water capturing and irrigation system was also installed to ensure resilience to drought. The project was implemented by the city district of Petržalka, which is - with 100,000 inhabitants - one of the larger and most densely populated districts of Bratislava. (1)

Focus

Creation of new green areas

Project objectives

The main goal of the project was to reconstruct the alleys on the Square of Freedom (Liberty Square). The sub-goals included, removal of 1 ha of the pavement and replace with grass, trees, and flower beds. To install water capturing and irrigation systems to ensure resilience to drought. In addition, installation of recreational areas (playground, climbing frames, recreational areas for adults). (1, 20)

Implementation activities

The first phase of implementation involves the felling of disruptive or poor health trees and root systems to make space for the implementation of the squares recreational and green areas. The second stage involves the implementation of said areas as well as restoration of the fountain's pipeline and the construction of water retention measures. Also, reconstruction of roads, sidewalks and passages and implementation of new cycle routes. (8, 9, 14)

Details on the roles of the organisations involved in the project

Bratislava City Office coordinates the pan-project named "Bratislava is preparing for climate change - pilot application of the measures in the field of sustainable rainwater". Apart from the city districts, cooperation on the project was sought with the Bratislava Regional Conservation Association, Bratislava Water Company, City Committee of Slovak Nature and Landscape Conservation Union, City Forest in Bratislava, Comenius University in Bratislava, Nature Sciences Faculty to involve them in the plans. The Norwegian partner COWI was involved because of its experience in the field and to underline the spirit of the EEA Grants and Norway Grants regarding international cooperation. (1, 3, 5, 6) Subsequent budgetary limitations to the project's involvement with this NBS led to the involvement of property developer HB Reavis in May 2017. (8)
